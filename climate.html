3Ô∏è‚É£ Effects of Climate Change

The effects of climate change are already visible across the globe and are becoming more intense each year. Rising global temperatures have led to melting glaciers, sea level rise, and more frequent extreme weather events such as hurricanes, floods, and wildfires. In some regions, droughts are devastating agriculture, while others face record rainfall and flooding. Ecosystems are under stress; coral reefs are bleaching, polar bears are losing habitat, and migratory birds are changing patterns. Human health is at risk due to increased heatwaves, new disease vectors like mosquitoes spreading into new areas, and worsening air quality. Coastal cities are threatened by encroaching waters, and millions face displacement due to climate-induced disasters. Food and water security are in danger, especially in developing countries. Economies suffer as crops fail, infrastructure is damaged, and insurance losses rise
